Why watch only heat? ↓

the judgment

Do not monitor everything. Only what the AI cannot fix itself.

These arms are closed-loop: they watch the object and adapt, so they shrug off most hardware faults. Two filters (can the policy compensate? does it actually recur?) cut ten candidates down to one.

One proven fault, one labeled candidate, the rest cut. Every cut is documented; the next fault gets added when fleet data earns it.

what this is

The judgment is the work, not the feature count.

A health check for the robots that train AI policies. It catches when the robot, not the AI, is quietly corrupting the data, and it only tests the faults that actually do that: one proven (overheating) plus one labeled candidate. Knowing what not to build, and printing no number I cannot defend, is the point. Built end to end by Yash Prakash, with a tested Python core and this live simulation.
